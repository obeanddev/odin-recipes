Pork Osso Bucco

Description

A variation on the Osso Bucco traditional recipe in which pork replaces turkey.

This variation uses fennels and apples that makes the recipe convenient for sweet palates.

Ingredients

  • 6 thick-cut pork shank sections, 2 to 3 inches thick
  • black pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon table salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1 large yellow onion, sliced
  • 2 medium-sized fennels
  • 1 large carrot, diced
  • 1 glass red wine
  • 3 to 4 four medium medium-sized apples
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour

Steps

  1. Season pork shanks with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil and butter in a pot over medium heat. Sear the pork shanks one by one, two minutes on each side.Then done, set aside.
  3. Cook and stir onion,carrot, fennels and salt in the same pot at medium heat
  4. Add the flour ans stir to dissolve it.
  5. Add wine and simmer until sauce reduces slightly.
  6. Add the diced apples.
  7. Return the pork shanks to the pot.
  8. Cover and cook at low heat for three to three and an half hours.
  9. Serve with plenty of sauce and enjoy!
